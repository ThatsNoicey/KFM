Scientific Basis
----------------
The KFMS algorithm combines three biomechanical factors into a normalized score (0-100):

\[ \text{KFMS} = \left(\underbrace{\frac{\theta_{\text{actual}}}{\theta_{\text{target}} \alpha}_{\text{ROM}} + \underbrace{\frac{t_{\text{hold}}}{t_{\text{target}}} \beta}_{\text{Endurance}} + \underbrace{\text{SI} \gamma}_{\text{Control}} \right) \times \Gamma \]

Where:
- \(\theta_{\text{actual}} = \text{Maximum achieved flexion angle (degrees)}\)- \(\theta_{\text{target}} = \text{Clinical rehabilitation target (120° for adults)}\)- \(t_{\text{hold}} = \text{Time maintained at >80\% max angle (seconds)}\)- \(\text{SI} = \text{Stability Index} = \frac{\mu_\theta}{\theta_{\text{max}}} \left(1 - \frac{\sigma_\theta}{\theta_{\text{max}}}\right)\)- \(\Gamma = \text{Progression Factor} = 1 + \frac{\text{KFMS} - \text{Baseline KFMS}}{100}\)

Coefficients (\(lpha = 0.4, eta = 0.4, \gamma = 0.2\)) reflect clinical priorities balancing range of motion (ROM), endurance, and movement control. The progression factor rewards improvement while maintaining score interpretability.
